About AASIOM

Fieldwork before
technology.

AASIOM studies highway incidents, fleet operations, response delays, and claim-flow gaps before building technology. Our current focus is ANVIRA — a system direction for improving how highway incidents are reported, coordinated, and documented.

We start with the road, the driver, the fleet operator, the responder, the insurer, and the handoffs between them.

Incident → response → claim

A highway incident is not just one event. It is a chain of actions where every delayed call, missing photo, unclear location, or weak handoff can create operational and financial loss.

Why AASIOM Exists

The real delay is not only technical.

When a truck breaks down or meets with an accident, the first few minutes decide the quality of the entire response. But information often moves through phone calls, WhatsApp messages, unclear locations, and incomplete reports.

AASIOM exists to study this reality closely and build systems around it. We look at how incidents are reported, how fleets coordinate, how responders are activated, and how claim evidence is created.

How We Think

Clear Systems. Real People. Useful Technology.

AASIOM’s approach is simple: understand the problem properly, design around the people involved, and build technology only where it makes the system work better.

We Study the Real Situation First

Before building anything, we study how a highway incident actually happens: who reports it, who responds, what gets delayed, and where information is lost.

Field Research

We Design Around People

The system must work for drivers, fleet managers, responders, insurers, and survey teams. If they cannot use it under pressure, the technology has failed.

Human System

We Keep the Process Simple

AASIOM focuses on reducing confusion: clearer reporting, faster routing, better coordination, and cleaner information for claim-related decisions.

How We Handle Data

Data governance is part of trust.

AASIOM’s work may involve operational conversations, field observations, incident workflows, and stakeholder inputs. These details are handled carefully because data trust is part of the system.

We aim to collect only what is necessary for research and system understanding. Sensitive operational details should be handled with restraint, clear purpose, and controlled access.
