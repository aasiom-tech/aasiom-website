About AASIOM

Fieldwork before
technology.

AASIOM is an operational-research company studying highway incidents, fleet operations, and claim-flow coordination before building technology. Its current focus is ANVIRA — an incident-to-resolution system direction for highway freight.

We do not begin with a product claim. We begin with the road, the dispatcher, the driver, the responder, the insurer, and the handoffs between them.

Why AASIOM Exists

The delay is a coordination gap.

On the highway, the problem is rarely only about technology. A driver reports an incident, the fleet tries to understand what happened, assistance is called, the insurer enters later, and each handoff can lose time, location context, evidence, and accountability.

AASIOM was created to study these operating gaps directly. Before proposing a product, we examine how incidents are reported, who acts first, what information is lost, and where delays become operational or financial loss.

How We Think

Specificity Over Philosophy

AASIOM’s work is strongest when it names the road, the role, the handoff, the delay, and the decision point clearly.

Fieldwork Before Claims

We begin with observed field behaviour: who gets called, what information moves, where handoffs break, and which delays create real operational loss.

Operating Rule

People Before Interfaces

We design around the people who report, coordinate, assess, verify, dispatch, and resolve incidents under pressure.

Design Standard

Technology Comes Last

Only after the incident-to-claim chain is understood do we decide what software, automation, data flow, or intelligence layer should exist.

Build Logic

No Surveillance Framing

ANVIRA is not driver policing or citizen tracking. The focus is incident triage, response coordination, evidence structure, and operational clarity.

Trust Boundary

One Wedge First

We focus on one sharp wedge first: highway incidents, fleet downtime, response latency, and claim leakage.

Strategic Focus

Research-Led Decisions

Every decision begins with field observations, stakeholder conversations, workflow mapping, and operational evidence.

How We Handle Data

Data governance is part of trust.

AASIOM’s work may involve operational conversations, field observations, incident workflows, and stakeholder inputs. These details are handled carefully because data trust is part of the system.

We aim to collect only what is necessary for research and system understanding. Sensitive operational details are handled with restraint, and public insights should avoid exposing private, personal, or commercially sensitive information.

Consent Stakeholder inputs handled with clear research purpose.
DPDP Governance posture aligned with responsible data handling.
Minimal Only necessary operational data should be collected.
Controlled Access, retention, and sharing must be treated with discipline.
