We study how highway incidents really unfold before we write a line of code. Our 5-stage framework places operational reality before technology — credible to operators burned by vendors who build first and ask questions never.

Stage detail

Each stage: objective, activities, output

Concrete, not abstract. Each stage produces something specific before the next begins.

01. Research

Objective: Understand the exact timeline of a highway incident — from the driver's first call to the surveyor's arrival.

Activities: Riding with dispatchers, shadowing fleet control rooms, interviewing tow operators and surveyors on site.

Output: Detailed field notes mapping the first 30 minutes after a truck goes down — who gets called, in what order, where the time disappears.

02. Field Validation

Objective: Verify findings on active corridors with real stakeholders who live this problem daily.

Activities: Partnering with mid-size interstate fleets on the Western Express Highway; running design-partner pilots.

Output: Validated or falsified hypotheses — specific, dated, from one corridor. Not desk research.

03. Understanding

Objective: Identify where the delay actually originates — and why previous attempts at solving it failed.

Activities: Mapping coordination gaps, information loss points, and incentive misalignments across the incident-to-claim chain.

Output: The insight that the delay is a coordination gap, not a technology gap. Four handoffs lose minutes and information before a surveyor ever arrives.

04. Design

Objective: Blueprint a solution shaped entirely by what field observation revealed — not by what's technically easiest.

Activities: System architecture informed by real workflows; wireframes built around operator needs, not feature lists.

Output: ANVIRA's architecture — an incident-to-resolution system designed for the people who actually handle the first 30 minutes.

05. Technology

Objective: Build only what the understanding demands — not a feature set invented at a desk.

Activities: Building ANVIRA's incident capture, structured data pipeline, and stakeholder coordination layer.

Output: ANVIRA — an incident-to-resolution system for highway freight, currently in field validation on one Mumbai corridor.

What guides us

Six principles — shown once, for the whole site

These are the rules we hold ourselves to. Each stated once, with a concrete consequence — never a list of six repeated on three pages.

Reality over assumptions
We ride along, observe, and document — before we hypothesise. Every claim on this site comes from a corridor, not a conference room.
Specificity over philosophy
Every sentence must contain a number, a place, a named role, or a concrete object. If it could appear on a competitor's site unchanged, it isn't working.
Understanding before building
Technology is the fifth stage, not the first. Software that ignores operational reality creates new problems without solving old ones.
Honest staging
We state where we are: field validation on one corridor, pre-seed stage, with design partners. Zero deployment claims beyond what exists today.
Restraint over decoration
If an element feels like startup hype, AI marketing, or futuristic speculation — it is removed. Seriousness is signalled by refusing to shout.
Evidence over assertion
We publish what we learn as we learn it — dated, specific, from one corridor. For a research-first company with no logos, field insights are the credibility.
