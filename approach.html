Why it matters

Most tech companies skip straight to Stage 5

They build the software first, then realise the problem is different from what they imagined. Operators on the Western Express Highway have seen this before — vendors who arrive with dashboards built for offices, not for the first 30 minutes after a truck goes down.

AASIOM inverts this. We ride with dispatchers. We shadow tow operators. We interview surveyors who arrive to scenes that have already been cleared. Only when we understand the coordination gap do we design — and only after design do we build.

The result: ANVIRA is architected around how the incident actually unfolds, not how a product manager imagined it from a conference room.

The 5-stage framework

Each stage: objective, activities, output

Concrete, not abstract. Each stage produces something specific before the next begins. Technology is placed last — credible to operators burned by vendors.

01
Stage 01 — Research

Map the incident timeline before anyone builds anything

Field shadowing Dispatcher interviews Control room observation Timeline mapping
Objective Understand the exact timeline of a highway incident — from the driver's first call to the surveyor's arrival on scene.
Activities Riding with dispatchers on live shifts. Shadowing fleet control rooms during incident windows. Interviewing tow operators and surveyors on site — not in meeting rooms.
Output Detailed field notes mapping the first 30 minutes after a truck goes down — who gets called, in what order, and exactly where the time disappears.
02
Stage 02 — Field Validation

Test hypotheses on real corridors with real stakeholders

Western Express Highway Design-partner pilots Hypothesis testing Mid-size fleet partnerships
Objective Verify findings on active corridors with real stakeholders who live this problem daily — not consultants who've read about it.
Activities Partnering with mid-size interstate fleets on the Western Express Highway in Mumbai. Running structured design-partner pilots with dispatchers and ops managers.
Output Validated or falsified hypotheses — specific, dated, from one corridor. Not desk research. Not survey data. Live observations from operational ground truth.
03
Stage 03 — Understanding

Identify where the delay originates — and why past solutions failed

Coordination gap mapping Information loss points Incentive misalignments Chain analysis
Objective Identify where the delay actually originates — and why every previous attempt at solving it has failed to stick with operators.
Activities Mapping coordination gaps, information loss points, and incentive misalignments across the full incident-to-claim chain. Tracing who loses what data at each handoff.
Output The core insight: the ~23-minute delay is a coordination gap, not a technology gap. Four handoffs — Driver → Dispatcher → Tow Operator → Surveyor — each lose minutes and information before resolution.
04
Stage 04 — Design

Blueprint solutions entirely shaped by what field observation revealed

System architecture Operator-first wireframes Workflow-driven design Field-to-blueprint translation
Objective Blueprint a solution shaped entirely by what field observation revealed — not by what is technically easiest or what a product demo looks impressive with.
Activities System architecture informed by real workflows. Wireframes built around operator needs and dispatcher constraints — not feature lists assembled in isolation.
Output ANVIRA's architecture — an incident-to-resolution system designed for the people who handle the first 30 minutes on an active highway, not for the analyst who reviews it three days later.
05
Stage 05 — Technology

Build only what the understanding demands — nothing invented at a desk

ANVIRA build Incident capture Structured data pipeline Stakeholder coordination layer
Objective Build only what the understanding demands — not a feature set invented at a desk or assembled from a competitor's press release.
Activities Building ANVIRA's incident capture layer, structured data pipeline, and stakeholder coordination system — each component traceable back to a field observation.
Output ANVIRA — an incident-to-resolution system for highway freight, currently in field validation on the Western Express Highway corridor in Mumbai. Pre-seed stage. No deployment claims beyond what exists.
What guides us

Six operating principles

These are the rules we hold ourselves to — each stated once with a concrete consequence. Not a list of values that sound good on slides.

Reality over assumptions
We ride along, observe, and document before we hypothesise. Every claim on this site comes from a corridor, not a conference room.
Specificity over philosophy
Every sentence must contain a number, a place, a named role, or a concrete object. If it could appear on a competitor's site unchanged, it isn't working.
Understanding before building
Technology is the fifth stage, not the first. Software that ignores operational reality creates new problems without solving old ones.
Honest staging
We state where we are: field validation on one corridor, pre-seed stage, with design partners. Zero deployment claims beyond what exists today.
Restraint over decoration
If an element feels like startup hype or futuristic speculation, it is removed. Seriousness is signalled by refusing to shout.
Evidence over assertion
We publish what we learn as we learn it — dated, specific, from one corridor. For a research-first company with no logos yet, field insights are the only credibility.

What changes

What does "getting the approach right" actually produce?

When technology is placed last, the software that emerges is fundamentally different from what a product team invents from scratch.

  • Operators recognise it. When a tool is designed around what dispatchers actually do at 2 AM on the WEH, they don't need training slides. They recognise the workflow immediately.
  • The coordination gap closes. Not through technology alone — through technology that understands which handoff loses time and precisely intervenes there.
  • Claims data is captured at source. Because the system is present at the incident, structured data reaches the insurer with the scene — not 3 days after it was cleared.
