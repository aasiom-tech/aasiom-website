The problem

Why highway response is slow

On the highway, the delay isn't a technology gap — it's a coordination gap. Each handoff loses minutes and information before anyone with authority sees the scene.

Step 1
Driver calls the fleet
Step 2
Fleet calls a tow operator
Step 3
Someone eventually calls the insurer
Step 4
Surveyor arrives — scene is gone

By the time a surveyor sees it, the scene is gone and the claim is already inflated.

Field validation · Western Express Highway, Mumbai

ANVIRA: an incident-to-resolution system for highway freight

When a vehicle is involved in a highway incident, ANVIRA turns the first chaotic minutes into structured, time-stamped, verified information — compressing response from the ~23 minutes it takes today toward under a minute, and giving fleets and insurers a clean record that stops claim leakage.

How we work

Research before design. Technology last.

Five stages, in order, every time. The discipline reassures operators burned before by vendors who skipped straight to software.

01

Research

Mapping the operational reality of highway incidents before writing a line of code.

02

Field validation

Testing what we've learned with dispatchers, surveyors, and fleets on one corridor.

03

Operational understanding

Turning field observation into a model of how incidents actually resolve.

04

System design

Designing ANVIRA's pipeline around that model — not the other way round.

05

Technology

Built only once the operational picture is real. Technology comes last.
