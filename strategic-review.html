01 Executive Summary

AASIOM is narrowing its work around one operational gap.

AASIOM studies high-friction Indian transport operations where a single incident becomes many disconnected calls, updates, photos, documents, blockers, and decisions.

ANVIRA is the first focused product direction: a WhatsApp-first incident command workflow for Indian trucking fleets, built to turn driver alerts and scattered evidence into one controlled incident case file.

02 Director's Note

Director's Note

AASIOM's current work is focused on understanding where operational coordination breaks down inside Indian transport workflows.

ANVIRA is our answer to one specific problem: what happens after a trucking incident, when information starts moving through calls, WhatsApp messages, photos, locations, claim documents, and follow-ups.

We are not starting with a large platform promise. We are starting with one workflow, one industry, and one measurable customer problem.

The goal is simple: help fleet owners convert scattered incident information into one clear, usable case file.

Om Vinayak Muchandikar, Director
03 Why AASIOM Exists

Operational gaps become business loss.

Many transport problems are not only technology problems. They are workflow problems. When information is scattered, teams lose time, evidence quality falls, follow-up becomes unclear, and business decisions become slower.

AASIOM exists to study these operational gaps first, then design systems that make coordination clearer and more accountable.

Scattered activity

Calls, messages, photos, locations, documents, and verbal updates stay disconnected.

Structured case file

07 What ANVIRA Does

ANVIRA supports coordination and documentation.

Structure incident updates into one case file

Keep critical context organized instead of scattered.

Help collect and order evidence

Bring photos, videos, documents, and locations into a usable sequence.

Support fleet coordination and follow-up

Make ownership and next steps easier to track.

Track downtime blockers

Surface what is delaying repair, cargo movement, or claim preparation.

Flag cargo continuity in major cases

Help teams notice cases where cargo movement requires attention.

Generate an evidence dossier

Prepare a structured record for internal review and claim support.

08 What ANVIRA Does Not Do

ANVIRA does not replace critical services or decision-makers.

Guarantee insurance claim approval

Claim decisions remain with insurers and relevant authorities.

Decide legal liability

The product does not determine fault or legal responsibility.

Replace police, ambulance, or emergency services

Emergency response must remain with the appropriate service providers.

Provide towing or repair services itself

ANVIRA supports coordination, not service delivery.

Promise accident prevention

The focus is post-incident clarity and follow-up control.

Track vehicles like GPS software

ANVIRA is not a live fleet tracking product.

ANVIRA supports coordination and documentation. It does not replace emergency services, police, ambulance, towing, repair, or insurance decision-making.
09 Current Stage and Pilot Plan

Current stage: controlled demo and pilot preparation.

ANVIRA is currently being prepared as a controlled demo model using dummy data. It is not a public-use product and not yet a fully automated production system. The purpose is to validate workflow value with real fleet owners before building too much.

Demo data only

Current examples are designed for explanation and internal testing.

Fleet-owner feedback

The next step is to test whether the workflow matches real operating pain.

Pilot-readiness

Product depth will increase only after workflow value is validated.

10 Evidence, Trust, and Data Handling

Trust depends on careful evidence handling.

Controlled access

Access limited to authorized fleet users.

Purpose-limited use

Incident data serves the customer's workflow, nothing unrelated.

Timestamped records

Updates and evidence are organized in time order for later review.

Evidence integrity

Collected items are kept organized and traceable within the case file.

Customer authorization

Data handling happens under the fleet customer's authorization.

Privacy-aware design

Designed with Indian data protection expectations in mind as the company matures.

11 Customer Proof We Need

The next proof must come from fleet operators.

Does the workflow match real incident handling?

Fleet owners must confirm that the sequence reflects actual field pressure.

Does one case file reduce confusion?

The product must make coordination clearer than current calls and chats.

Does evidence become easier to review?

The dossier must support internal review and claim-readiness work.

Does downtime visibility improve?

Teams should see blockers earlier and with better ownership.

Will fleet teams actually use it?

The workflow must fit daily behavior, not force unnatural adoption.

What should be automated next?

Automation should follow proven workflow demand, not assumptions.
