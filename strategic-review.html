01 Executive Summary

AASIOM is narrowing its work around one operational gap.

AASIOM studies high-friction Indian transport operations where a single incident becomes many disconnected calls, updates, photos, documents, blockers, and decisions.

ANVIRA is the first focused product direction: a WhatsApp-first incident command workflow for Indian trucking fleets, built to turn driver alerts and scattered evidence into one controlled incident case file.

One wedge

Post-incident coordination for trucking fleets comes before broader product expansion.

One workflow

Driver alert, evidence capture, next actions, blockers, and dossier generation stay in one case file.

One stage

ANVIRA is in controlled demo and pilot preparation, not public-scale deployment.

One standard

Claims must stay precise, readable, and honest about what ANVIRA does and does not do.

02 Director's Note

Director's Note

AASIOM's current work is focused on one question: can we make the first operational response after a trucking incident more structured, documented, and accountable?

ANVIRA is our answer to that question.

We are not starting with a broad fleet-management platform. We are starting with the part of the workflow where confusion is highest and proof is most fragile: the first report, the evidence trail, the internal decisions, and the follow-up actions.

The goal for this phase is simple: build a controlled demo, place it in front of fleet owners, learn from real operational feedback, and improve only what makes the workflow clearer and more useful.

Om Vinayak Muchandikar, Director
03 Why AASIOM Exists

Operational gaps become business loss.

In Indian transport operations, the problem is rarely only one accident, one document, or one phone call. The real loss comes from scattered information, unclear ownership, delayed follow-up, missing evidence, and decisions made without a complete operational picture.

AASIOM exists to convert these fragmented workflows into structured systems that improve visibility, accountability, and documentation discipline.

04 Why ANVIRA Is First

AASIOM starts where incident coordination breaks down fastest.

ANVIRA is first because post-incident fleet coordination has visible operational pain, repeatable workflow steps, and clear evidence requirements. It is narrow enough to validate carefully and important enough to create practical value if the workflow works.

High-friction moment

Accidents, vehicle damage, driver alerts, cargo disruption, and claim documentation converge quickly.

Clear workflow wedge

Useful pilot test

05 The Problem: Post-Accident Chaos

Truck incidents do not fail only at the scene. They fail in the follow-up.

The first call is unclear.

Driver reports are often incomplete, emotional, or missing key details.

Evidence is scattered.

Photos, location, documents, and updates remain spread across WhatsApp and calls.

Duties are unclear.

Owners, managers, surveyors, and internal teams work from different information.

The vehicle stays blocked.

Repair, tow assignment, and route decisions can remain unresolved.

The cargo decision is delayed.

Cargo continuity risk needs visibility before it becomes a separate problem.

There is no single case file.

The incident story becomes a chain of calls, chats, and missed context.

06 ANVIRA Incident Control Stack

From driver alert to evidence dossier.

ANVIRA's workflow is shown as 13 layers grouped into four phases.

Capture
  1. Driver incident intake
  2. WhatsApp-style guided update collection
  3. Severity triage
  4. Location and vehicle context
  5. Photo/video/document evidence capture
Structure
  1. Incident case file creation
  2. Task owner assignment
Control
  1. Downtime blocker tracking
  2. Cargo continuity alert
  3. Claim-readiness checklist
Deliver
  1. Evidence dossier PDF generation
  2. Timeline and follow-up audit trail
  3. Fleet owner dashboard
The dashboard is not the product. The workflow beneath it is the product.

09 Current Stage and Pilot Plan

Current stage: controlled demo and pilot preparation.

ANVIRA is currently being prepared as a controlled demo model using dummy data. It is not a public-use product and not yet a fully automated production system. The purpose is to validate workflow value with real fleet owners before building too much.

Demo data only

No real driver, vehicle, accident, FIR, insurance, or customer records should appear in the demo.

Fleet-owner feedback

The next step is review with operators who face repeated incident coordination challenges.

Pilot-readiness decisions

Feedback should define what belongs in a narrow pilot and what should wait.

10 Evidence, Trust, and Data Handling

Trust depends on careful evidence handling.

Controlled access

Access limited to authorized fleet users.

Purpose-limited use

Incident data serves the customer's workflow, nothing unrelated.

Timestamped records

Updates and evidence are organized in time order for later review.

Evidence integrity

Collected items are kept organized and traceable within the case file.

Customer authorization

Data handling happens under the fleet customer's authorization.

Privacy-aware design

Designed with Indian data protection expectations in mind as the company matures.
